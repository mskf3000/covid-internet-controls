wonderful experience, a few rough edges
My wife and I wanted to have a birding excursion in Ghana. We chose Easy-Track because it was reasonably priced and also had excellent reviews on tripadvisor. Also, their tours are flexible so as to cater to individual interests. We had Jessie as our driver, who was very capable, and a comfortable vehicle for travel. Kwami Brown was our birding guide. Kwami Brown is a super guide, passionate about birds, and eager to help you to see as many as possible and to get good photos. He is excellent in spotting birds, and identifying them quickly by their call or song or silhouette. We were very pleased with many of the accommodations that Easy-Track arranged for us, in Accra (Coconut Grove Sakumono Hotel), in Keta (Aborigenes Beach Resort), in Atimpoka (Aylos Bay Guest House) and in Koforidua (Mac-Dic Royal Plaza Hotel). Our next stop was the Bobiri Guest House. There is no wifi and no electricity, except from 6 PM to 10 PM (from a generator). We stayed there 2 nights. One would have been enough, as we were only birding in the Bobiri Reserve in the early morning and late afternoon and went on other excursions during the day. We could have had access to the reserve in the early morning without lodging there. After our second morning of birding in Bobiri we want to the Owabi Wildlife Sanctuary, and from there we drove to Cape Coast. We would have had nice accommodations there at the Oasis Beach Resort, but when we arrived at 8 PM they had given away our room. Jessie found other accommodations for us but they were very basic. When we awoke in the morning there was no electricity. The breakfast menu listed many items but hardly any were available and they took a long time to come. Our next stop was Kakum National Park, where we were to stay in the Tree House for two nights. One was enough. We figured that the tree house would be quaint, with a nice opportunity for viewing birds from the treetops, a little adventure for us, but it was a disappointment. A park guide leads you on a long trek to the tree house at the end of the afternoon when light is waning. So we saw no birds on the way and arrived there soaked with perspiration. There is no water and no electricity, just one large circular low roofed room with no chair or furnishings. A wire mesh covers the openings all around the room, so we could not have taken a photo of a bird had we seen one (which we didn't). We had thin mattresses for sleeping and a mosquito net (we did not see a single mosquito in Ghana in January). The outhouse was down a pathway in the woods. So one night was enough. Jessie found some nice accommodations nearby for the next night (Rainforest Hotel). Back near Accra for our final night, we stayed at the Kokobrite Gardens, which was very nice. I have included some of the details about our more rustic accommodations as Easy Track is flexible and if you might feel about some of these accommodations in the same way that we did, you might be able to arrange for other lodgings. Easy Track does cultural excursions as well as birding excursions.
